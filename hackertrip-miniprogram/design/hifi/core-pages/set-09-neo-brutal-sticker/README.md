## Why This Direction

This direction tests whether HackerTrip can feel like a young builder community rather than a conventional productivity app. Neo-brutal components use bold outlines, hard shadows, sticker labels, and punchy color blocks, which makes hackathon content feel more alive.

## Product Logic

- Thick borders make interactive modules highly scannable.
- Sticker-like event cards make each hackathon feel collectible.
- Strong yellow/red/cyan accents create energy without relying on gradients.
- The design is intentionally louder than the first 8 sets, useful for testing brand memorability.

## Fit For HackerTrip

It fits early adopter communities, student hackathons, creator-developer events, and social sharing around identity cards. It can make HackerTrip stand out immediately.

## Risk

The style may feel too playful for enterprise AI competitions or serious schedule management. It needs careful discipline to avoid visual noise.

## Best Use

Use as a brand-forward exploration if HackerTrip wants to own a youthful, expressive, community-native identity.
