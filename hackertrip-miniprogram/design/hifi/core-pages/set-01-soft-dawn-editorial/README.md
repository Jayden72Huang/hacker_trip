## Why This Direction

This direction treats HackerTrip as a calm AI companion rather than a dense event feed. The soft dawn palette lowers friction for first-time users who may not yet know which hackathon to join. It is suitable for the AI-matching entry flow because it feels approachable and reflective.

## Product Logic

- Background uses pale blue and blush to keep the first screen quiet.
- Peach is reserved for deadlines, CTA, and identity-card generation.
- Cards remain white and spacious so the event information stays readable.
- The emotional tone supports "tell AI what I want" better than a pure developer-console style.

## Fit For HackerTrip

Best for positioning HackerTrip as a friendly AI participation companion. It supports discovery, chat, and identity-card generation without making the product feel too enterprise or too technical.

## Risk

The palette is softer than typical hackathon/AI products. If HackerTrip needs to look more technical and founder-facing, this direction may feel too lifestyle-oriented.

## Recommendation

Use this if the first launch prioritizes onboarding, student users, and broad accessibility.
